Project 1: Google Workspace Mastery

Analysis: Recognized that the district operates as a Google Workspace district, but many teachers lacked understanding and training on how to effectively utilize its tools.

Design: Created a structured training video focusing on Google Docs, Slides, Sheets, and Forms to address this gap and provide practical, classroom-focused solutions.

Development: Designed examples showcasing collaborative documents, interactive presentations, automated grade tracking, and self-grading quizzes to demonstrate the platform's potential.

Implementation: Delivered the training via a video demonstration. Due to student privacy, the video cannot be shared as it includes student names.

Evaluation: Collected feedback from educators to refine the training and ensure it effectively supports classroom efficiency and enhances teaching workflows.

Project 2: Schoology Rundown

Analysis: Identified that while Schoology is the primary Learning Management System (LMS) used in the district, many educators struggle with its advanced features, limiting its potential to enhance student learning experiences.

Design: Developed a comprehensive training plan to familiarize educators with Schoology's features, focusing on creating, organizing, and managing classroom materials efficiently. The training included step-by-step guides and hands-on activities.

Development: Prepared guided LMS tours, hands-on activities, and resource materials tailored to educators’ specific needs. These resources emphasized practical application and were designed to support real-time problem-solving during the training sessions.

Implementation: Delivered the training program through in-person workshops and personalized one-on-one sessions. Participants were guided step-by-step, enabling them to gain confidence in navigating Schoology while addressing individual questions and concerns in real-time.

Evaluation: Collected direct feedback during one-on-one sessions and group workshops to measure training effectiveness. Adjustments were made based on participant input to ensure the training met their diverse needs and improved their ability to integrate Schoology into daily teaching practices.

Project 3: Classroom engagement tools

Analysis: Identified a need for more engaging and interactive lesson delivery methods in classrooms to improve student participation and enhance learning outcomes. Teachers were unfamiliar with tools like Nearpod, Pear Deck, and Flippity and how they could be effectively integrated into their curriculum.

Design: Developed a training program featuring live demonstrations and hands-on activities to showcase the capabilities of Nearpod, Pear Deck, and Flippity. The program was designed to empower teachers to create their own interactive content tailored to their subjects and teaching styles.

Development: Prepared training materials, including live demo content, step-by-step guides, and example lesson plans. The resources emphasized how to leverage the unique features of each tool—such as interactive slides, gamified activities, and real-time feedback—while aligning with curriculum goals.

Implementation:Delivered the program through in-person workshops where teachers participated in live demonstrations, followed by guided sessions to create and test their own interactive lessons. Each participant received personalized support to ensure they felt confident integrating these tools into their teaching practices.

Evaluation: Gathered feedback from teachers on their experience using the tools and implementing them in their classrooms. Success was measured by increased teacher confidence and early classroom use of the tools, with follow-ups to address any challenges or advanced training needs.

Project 4: The Teacher's Assistant: AI in the Classroom

Analysis: Identified a growing interest among educators in leveraging AI tools to support teaching and classroom management but noted a lack of understanding about practical applications and available tools.

Design: Developed an interactive training program to introduce educators to AI-driven tools such as tutoring systems, lesson-planning assistants, and administrative aids. The program included live demonstrations, case study reviews, and a Q&A session tailored to attendees' needs.

Development:Curated a list of AI tools and created engaging demonstrations to showcase their functionalities, such as automating lesson plans, generating personalized student feedback, and simplifying administrative tasks. Designed case studies highlighting successful implementations of AI in various educational settings.

Implementation:Delivered the program through live workshops. Participants experienced tool demonstrations, reviewed real-world applications of AI in education, and engaged in an interactive Q&A session to explore their specific concerns and use cases.

Evaluation: Collected feedback from attendees through surveys and real-time discussions to assess their understanding of AI tools and readiness to implement them. Adjusted the program to provide follow-up resources and additional training where needed.

Project 5: Classroom Management with Technology

Analysis: Recognized a need for teachers to improve communication with students and parents and effectively monitor student behavior using modern technology. Tools like Remind and GoGuardian were available but underutilized due to limited training.

Design: Created a training program that introduced teachers to the features and functionalities of Remind and GoGuardian. The program focused on account setup, exploring key features, and implementing best practices for fostering a positive classroom environment.

Development: Developed hands-on activities, including guided account setup, feature demonstrations, and interactive scenarios. Designed resources such as quick-start guides and best practices checklists to help teachers integrate these tools seamlessly into their daily routines.

Implementation: Delivered the training through workshops where teachers created accounts, explored the apps’ features in real-time, and participated in discussions about effective classroom management strategies. One-on-one support was provided to address individual questions and challenges.

Evaluation: Collected feedback through surveys and follow-up discussions to assess how effectively teachers were able to use the tools and apply best practices in their classrooms. Adjusted training materials based on feedback to provide ongoing support and advanced usage tips.

Project 6: Canva: Everyone's best friend

Analysis: Identified that educators frequently sought ways to create visually appealing and professional classroom materials but often lacked the design skills or tools to do so effectively. Canva was recognized as a versatile and user-friendly solution.

Design: Developed a workshop focused on introducing Canva as a tool for creating engaging classroom materials. The program included a live demo, hands-on design activities, and peer feedback sessions to enhance learning and creativity.

Development: Prepared a live demonstration on creating a classroom poster or presentation, along with guided resources like templates and step-by-step tutorials. Designed collaborative activities to encourage participants to explore Canva’s features and apply them to their own projects.

Implementation: Delivered an in-person workshop where participants created their own Canva designs with real-time support. Sessions included interactive feedback opportunities for participants to share their creations and receive constructive input from peers.

Evaluation: Collected participant feedback through surveys and group discussions to assess their confidence in using Canva and the quality of their creations. Adjusted the training content based on feedback to include more advanced tips and additional resources for ongoing support.

Project 7: Integrating Multimedia in the Classroom

Analysis: Identified that teachers wanted to incorporate multimedia elements like videos, podcasts, and graphics into their lessons to engage students, but many lacked experience with tools like Screencastify, Flipgrid, and Canva.

Design: Created a training program to guide educators in using multimedia tools to enhance their lessons. The program included demonstrations, hands-on content creation activities, and strategies for embedding multimedia into classroom materials.

Development: Developed step-by-step guides, templates, and examples to showcase the use of Screencastify for video creation, Flipgrid for interactive discussions, and Canva for designing graphics. Designed activities for participants to create their own multimedia content, such as video introductions, podcast episodes, and custom visuals.

Implementation: Delivered the training through workshops featuring hands-on creation sessions. Participants learned to use multimedia tools, embed videos and podcasts into their lessons, and design visually engaging graphics with real-time support and feedback.

Evaluation: Gathered feedback from participants through surveys and group discussions to evaluate their confidence in using the tools and the effectiveness of their multimedia-enhanced lessons. Adjusted training materials to address common challenges and included additional advanced tips and resources.
